Shawarma


Description

Inredients

For the Chicken & Marinade

  • Boneless, skinless chicken thighs: 1 kg (highly preferred over breasts for maximum juiciness)
  • Olive oil: 3 tablespoons
  • Lemon juice: 2 tablespoons (freshly squeezed)
  • Garlic cloves: 4 large, minced
  • Ground cumin: 1 tablespoon
  • Ground coriander: 1 tablespoon
  • Ground cardamom: 1 tablespoo
  • Smoked paprika:2 teaspoons
  • Salt:2 teaspoons
  • Ground cayenne pepper:1 teaspooon (halve to reduce heat)
  • Ground cinnamon:½ teaspoon
  • Black pepper:½ teaspoon

Quick Garlic Yogurt Sauce

  • Greek yogurt:1 cup (plain, full-fat)
  • Mayonnaise:⅓ cup
  • Garlic clove: 1 large, finely minced
  • Lemon juice:1 teaspoon
  • Salt and black pepper:To taste

For Serving

  • Flatbreads:4 to 6 large pitas, pita pockets, or Lebanese flatbreads
  • Vegetables:Sliced iceberg lettuce, ripe tomatoes, and finely sliced red onions
  • Pickles: Tangy pickled cucumbers or wild cucumbers

Steps

Marinate the Chicken

  1. Whisk together the olive oil, lemon juice, minced garlic, and all dry spices in a large bowl.
  2. Cut the chicken thighs horizontally into thin, even strips to optimize browning.
  3. Toss the chicken in the spice mixture until every piece is completely coated.
  4. Cover and refrigerate for at least 3 hours

Whip u the Sauce

  1. Combine the Greek yogurt, mayonnaise, minced garlic, and lemon juice in a small bowl.
  2. Season with a pinch of salt and pepper.Season with a pinch of salt and pepper.
  3. Cover and chill in the fridge until assembly time.

Cook to Perfection

  1. Stovetop:Heat a large cast-iron skillet over medium-high heat with one tablespoon of oil. Cook the chicken pieces in a single layer without overcrowding the pan. Sear for 4 to 5 minutes on the first side until deeply browned, flip, and cook for another 3 minutes.Heat a large cast-iron skillet over medium-high heat with one tablespoon of oil. Cook the chicken pieces in a single layer without overcrowding the pan. Sear for 4 to 5 minutes on the first side until deeply browned, flip, and cook for another 3 minutes.
  2. Oven: Spread the chicken strips evenly across a parchment-lined baking sheet. Bake in a preheated oven at 220°C (425°F) for 20 to 25 minutes. Switch to the broiler settings on high for 3 minutes to get crispy, charred edges.

Rest and Shred

  1. Transfer the cooked chicken to a cutting board and cover loosely with foil.
  2. Let it rest for 5 minutes so the juices redistribute instead of running out.
  3. Shave or slice the meat into very thin strips.
  4. Drizzle any leftover pan juices directly back over the shredded meat.
